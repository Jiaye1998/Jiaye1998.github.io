About

Turning hard problems into working results.

I turn hard physical-science problems into working results, and increasingly into AI and automation that make discovery faster. I work at the intersection of nanophotonics, rare-earth materials, and machine learning, and I’ve taken projects from first synthesis all the way to first-author Nature papers.

Currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Molecular Foundry, Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory (with Dr. Emory Chan and Dr. Bruce Cohen), working on photon upconversion, photon avalanche, and rare-earth nanomaterials. During my PhD at the National University of Singapore (with Prof. Xiaogang Liu and Prof. Qian He), I pushed optical nonlinearities beyond 500× through sublattice reconstruction, a problem many considered intractable (Nature, 2025).

What I can bring to a team:

  • Solving problems others can’t. I go after intractable questions and stay with them until they ship. Track record: 15+ publications, multiple in Nature / Nature Photonics.
  • AI and automation for real experiments. I build multi-agent LLM pipelines for scientific data extraction and open-source research tools, and work toward self-driving labs that close the design, make, and measure loop for materials.
  • Delivers under pressure. I run several projects in parallel and hit deadlines, whether it’s a manuscript, a review, or shipping software.
  • Collaborative and generous. I do my best work in cross-disciplinary teams; I peer-review, lead discussions, and like making other people’s work better.
Research directions

Photon upconversion & nonlinear photophysics

Energy transfer and ultrahigh optical nonlinearity through single-particle imaging and time-resolved analysis — toward super-resolution imaging, ultrasensitive sensing, and quantum photonics.

Nanomaterials synthesis & characterization

Functional nanostructures by conventional and automated high-throughput synthesis, with electron microscopy and synchrotron spectroscopy to link synthesis, structure, and property.

AI-enabled materials discovery

Domain-grounded LLM agents, machine-learning models, and rate-equation frameworks that understand lanthanide energy transfer and accelerate discovery.
